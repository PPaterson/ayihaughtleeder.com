A showcase from the University of Sydney account

Uni Retention Town Hall 25 March 2026 Phillip Paterson

Before we dive in.

The University of Sydney is one of our largest university accounts and a CAG client. The volume and complexity of their travel programme gives us a rich data set to work with.


What you're about to see are bespoke slide ideas I've been building into their QBRs. They're examples of what's possible — not a template you're required to use.


I wouldn't put this level of bespoke work into a smaller account like Western Sydney Uni, for instance. But for the right client, these kinds of insights can be a real differentiator at review time.

Think of these as a menu of ideas you can pick from when the account warrants it.
01

Operations &
Traveller Satisfaction

Combining CSAT/NPS data, named agent shout-outs, and real traveller feedback — both the wins and the friction points — into a single view.

Operations Update — Traveller Satisfaction Snapshot

Highlights: CSAT 4.2/5.0, NPS +30, named agents Jen V, Jess, Jordan & Keeley. Offline NPS +50 vs OBT NPS -20. Concur platform ease-of-use rated lowest at 3.7/5.

Operations Update — Traveller Voice

Two columns: what travellers love (responsiveness, helpfulness) vs where friction lives (Concur navigation, lack of training, needing a human component).

02

Fare Selection &
Purchase Behaviour

Diving into domestic and international fare classes, airline breakdown, cost-per-mile, and advance purchase patterns to find savings opportunities.

Domestic Fare Selection

2,720 restricted economy flights at $205 avg ($0.52/mi). Breakdown by Qantas (47.6%), Virgin (22.5%), Rex. Flexible economy and business class comparison.

International Airline Selection

Four key routes: Europe Business ($11K Qantas), LA Economy ($3K), Tokyo Economy ($2.4K), London Economy ($2.5K). Alternatives shown with potential quarterly savings.

Advance Purchase Analysis

Domestic: 33% booked 0-13 days out at $0.69/mi vs 39% at 29+ days at $0.53/mi. International: 67% booked 29+ days ahead — good discipline. Clear cost savings from booking earlier domestically.

03

Benchmarking vs
FCM Uni Average

Hotel attachment rates and online booking tool usage compared against the FCM university benchmark. These slides come from the University Review Maxx 7000 Generator I shared in the Uni Teams chat recently.

Hotel Attachment Rate — USYD vs FCM Uni Average

USYD domestic 40% vs 58% avg (-18 pts). International 16% vs 44% avg (-28 pts). A clear opportunity area to discuss with the client. Slide generated by the Maxx 7000 — BYO data.

Online Booking Tool Usage — USYD vs FCM Uni Average

USYD domestic OBT 77% (on par with avg). International 33% vs 14% avg (+19 pts) — strong result. Also from the Maxx 7000. Tim & Alicia are working on getting us better benchmark data.

The Maxx 7000 Generator

The Hotel Attachment and OBT Usage benchmark slides are generated by the University Review Maxx 7000 tool I shared in the Uni Teams chat recently.


It's a BYO data tool — you plug in the client's rates and the FCM uni average, and it outputs branded comparison slides ready to drop into your QBR deck.

Tim & Alicia are working on getting us consistent benchmark data across the uni portfolio, which will make this even easier to use. Watch that space.
04

Proactive Value &
Industry Insights

Showing clients we're looking out for them — from Qantas distribution surcharges to pre-ticketing change fee analysis to real savings stories.

Qantas Distribution Surcharge Impact

$11.50/flight surcharge on GDS bookings from Jul 2025. FCM as Premium NDC Partner exempt. USYD still on legacy Concur/EDIFACT = $36,697 in fees across 3,191 Qantas flights. Strong reason to move to NDC-enabled platform.

Pre-Ticketing Change Fees

67 domestic/trans-Tasman and 600 international pre-ticketing change fees charged. 88 bookings had 3+ fees. One London-Athens return had 9 fees alone. Highlights traveller behaviour patterns to address.

Value Add of the Quarter

Jen Hugh saved $10,288 on a Manila booking by contacting Qantas and securing authority to ticket at the original expired fare before the price increase hit. Concrete example of agent value.
