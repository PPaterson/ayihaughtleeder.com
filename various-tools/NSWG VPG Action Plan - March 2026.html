Overview

A strong partnership
with room to grow.

The message from the room was clear: agencies genuinely like working with FCM. The operations team came in for serious praise, FCM Platform is a big step up from what came before, and the account management and operations teams are building real relationships. That said, agencies also told us where we can do better. Mostly around comms, reporting, and a more consultative approach. Below is what we heard, grouped into three pillars, with a plan for what to do next.

Findings by Pillar

What the room told us.

Pillar 01

Technology

FCM Platform is well-received across the board. Agencies find it more user-friendly and customised than anything they've had before. Training tiles, airline credits, and SSO login are all working well. Where agencies want more is around greater communication before changes go live, reporting flexibility, hotel content accuracy, and giving agencies more self-service capability.

Strength

FCM Platform is well-liked across the board. Agencies find it user-friendly, customised, and a clear upgrade from the previous portal.

Strength

Training tiles, airline credit visibility, and SSO login are all working well. Out-of-policy hotel prompts are a hit with larger agencies.

Opportunity

Greater communication ahead of technology changes. Agencies want a heads-up before updates go live, not after they discover them.

Opportunity

Tidy up hotel data in reports, speed up THN-to-Serko content refresh, and give agencies more flexible, self-service reporting options.

Pillar 02

Operations

This was the feel-good story of the session. Feedback for operations was overwhelmingly positive. Agencies praised the responsiveness, the extra mile during disruptions, and the Code Orange response as a real standout. Monthly operations updates are valued and FCM has become an essential step in emergency booking processes. The areas to tighten up are around a more consultative approach, clearer fee invoicing, and some process gaps like property exclusion lists.

Strength

Overwhelmingly positive across the board. Consultants are friendly, responsive, and go the extra mile during disruptions and emergencies.

Strength

Code Orange response and productive comms during disruptions were specifically praised. FCM is now an essential step in emergency booking processes.

Opportunity

Create a simple guide on how agencies can exclude or flag properties. This came up across multiple agencies and would benefit everyone.

Opportunity

Sort out delayed charges. A few agencies flagged fees turning up months later without enough detail to reconcile them.

Pillar 03

Account Management

The bigger agencies all had good things to say about their AMs and the regular cadence of catch-ups, reviews, and updates. Agencies are happy with both account management and operations support. Training Bytes were complimented by every agency. Where we need to lift is making sure smaller agencies get the same love, and turning insights into actionable recommendations agencies can act on.

Strength

Agencies are happy with their AMs and the regular cadence of catch-ups, reviews, and updates.

Strength

Training Bytes are a hit. Every agency complimented them and want to see more regular engagement and updates.

Opportunity

Bring smaller agencies into the fold. Make sure they're getting regular reviews and not feeling left out of the conversation.

Opportunity

Move from reporting to recommending. Agencies want to know how they can save money and improve, not just see the numbers. Clear communication before and after reviews.

Where We Start

  • •Explore after-hours callback options, rideshare services, and transfer alternatives for agencies
