How to Use These Cards

Draw a card when a corporate travel vendor solution is live, contractually correct, and behaviourally wrong.

These cards emerged from repeated exposure to corporate travel programs after the pitch, after the contract, and after the go-live—when the story meets the system.

Some insights were recognised in hindsight, once delivery replaced ambition. Others surfaced mid-implementation, usually when a "standard workflow" encountered a real client, a real market, or a real deadline. A few were articulated intentionally, most often while explaining why something works in theory but not in practice.

The cards may be held collectively, as a running counterweight to roadmaps, sales narratives, and "directionally correct" reporting. They may also be used individually: draw one when progress stalls, confidence exceeds evidence, or the solution feels suspiciously familiar.

When a card is drawn, trust it—even if it contradicts the PowerPoint deck. Especially if it contradicts the PowerPoint deck.

These cards are not best practice. They are not commitments, features, or guarantees. They exist in the gaps between what was sold, what was built, and what is quietly being worked around.

They are intentionally unfinished. As platforms are rebranded, integrations deferred, and future states redefined, some ideas will age out and others will become unavoidable.

If the cards feel uncomfortable, they're probably accurate. If ignored, they will resurface later—wearing the label "known limitation."
